The Three Investment Pillars

The recommended 50 / 30 / 20 allocation prioritises near-term risk reduction through infrastructure while building long-run economic competitiveness via climate-tech and protecting the most vulnerable communities.

Coastal & Flood Infrastructure

$2.5B 50%
  • Seawall hardening (Cat 4, 50-yr horizon)
  • Stormwater drainage & pump station upgrades
  • Wetland restoration — 500K acres target
  • Buried utilities in high-risk zones

Climate-Tech Ecosystem

$1.5B 30%
  • Incubator grants & R&D commercialisation
  • NextEra grid hardening + storage
  • Attract 10+ climate-tech companies
  • $149B global market by 2032

Community & Equity Programs

$1.0B 20%
  • Workforce development — 50K jobs
  • Insurance affordability programs
  • Flood resilience grants for 2M+ residents
  • Investment proportional to SVI

Regional Allocation Methodology

Investment is distributed across the three target regions using a composite weight derived from population share, coastal exposure miles, percentage of land in FEMA flood zones, and CDC/ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index. The 50/30/20 pillar split is then applied within each region. Southeast Florida receives the largest share due to its population density and coastal risk concentration.
